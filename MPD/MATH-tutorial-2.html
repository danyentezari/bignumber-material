4.2 Formal Example

Definition: Let A and B be two sets. A binary relation from A to
B is a subset of a Cartesian product A x B.

  • Let R⊆A×BR \subseteq A \times BR⊆A×B means R is a set of ordered pairs of the form (a,b)(a,b)(a,b) where a∈Aa \in Aa∈A and b∈Bb \in Bb∈B.
  • We use the notation aRba R baRb to denote (a,b)∈R(a,b) \in R(a,b)∈R and aRba \sout{R} baRb to denote (a,b)∉R(a,b) \notin R(a,b)∈/R. If aRba R baRb, we say a is related to b by R.
