### Logarithms


| x             | $\log(x)$     |
|---------------|---------------|
| 1             | 0             |
| 10            | 1             |
| 100           | 2             |
| 1000          | 3             |              


Used for multiplying numbers more quickly
> $8 \times 128 = 2^3 \times 2^7 = 2 ^ {10} = 1024$

Log base 2 approximation
> $\log_2(1) = 0$
> $\log_2(2) = 1$
> $\log_2(3) = 1.585$
> $\log_2(4) = 2$
> $\log_2(5) = 2.322$

Using natural logarithms
> $ \log_2(x) = \dfrac{\ln(x)}{\ln(2)} = \ln(x) - \ln(2) = \dfrac{\log_e(x)}{\log_e(2)}$



$\log_e(n) = \ln(n)$
