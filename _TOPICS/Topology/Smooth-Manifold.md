# Smooth Manifold

<i>

**definition [d]** (*Smooth Manifold = Differentiable Manifold = $C^{\infty}$ Manifold*) An $n$-dimensional topological manifold equipped with a maximal smooth atlas: a second-countable Hausdorff space that is locally Euclidean, together with an atlas of charts whose transition maps are smooth.

- $(M, \mathcal{A})$ with $\mathcal{A}$ a maximal $C^{\infty}$ atlas .

where

- locally Euclidean: every point has a neighborhood homeomorphic to an open set in $\mathbb{R}^{n}$.
- Hausdorff and second-countable are the standard topological hypotheses.
- “maximal” means every compatible smooth chart is already in $\mathcal{A}$.

</i>

<i>

**definition [d]** (*Smooth Manifold = Differentiable Manifold = $C^{\infty}$ Manifold*) A topological manifold $M$ with an atlas $\{(U_{\alpha}, \varphi_{\alpha})\}$ such that every transition map

- $\varphi_{\beta} \circ \varphi_{\alpha}^{-1} : \varphi_{\alpha}(U_{\alpha} \cap U_{\beta}) \rightarrow \varphi_{\beta}(U_{\alpha} \cap U_{\beta})$

is infinitely differentiable ($C^{\infty}$).

where

- each $\varphi_{\alpha}: U_{\alpha} \rightarrow \mathbb{R}^{n}$ is a coordinate chart.
- $C^{\infty}$ transitions make derivatives of functions and tensors chart-independent.
- equivalent names: differentiable manifold, $C^{\infty}$ manifold.

</i>
