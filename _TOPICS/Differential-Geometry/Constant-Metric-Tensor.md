# Constant Metric Tensor

<i>

**definition [d]** (*Constant Metric Tensor = Metric with Constant Components*) A metric tensor $g_{\mu\nu}$ whose components do not depend on the coordinates in a given chart:

- $\partial_{\lambda} g_{\mu\nu} = 0$ .

where

- $g_{\mu\nu}$ is the metric in coordinates $x^{\lambda}$.
- $\partial_{\lambda} = \partial / \partial x^{\lambda}$.
- the prototype is the Minkowski metric $\eta_{\mu\nu}$ in inertial Cartesian coordinates.

</i>

<i>

**definition [d]** (*Constant Metric Tensor = Metric with Constant Components*) A metric tensor $g_{\mu\nu}$ that is independent of the coordinates in some chart,

- $\partial_{\lambda} g_{\mu\nu} = 0$ ,

so that the Christoffel symbols vanish identically:

- $\Gamma^{\rho}_{\ \mu\nu} = 0$ .

where

- $g_{\mu\nu}$ is the metric in coordinates $x^{\lambda}$.
- vanishing $\Gamma$ implies locally Minkowski / flat spacetime (Riemann $= 0$).
- the standard example is $\eta_{\mu\nu} = \operatorname{diag}(-1,1,1,1)$ in inertial coordinates.

</i>
