# Smooth

<i>

**definition [d]** (*Smooth = Infinitely Differentiable = $C^{\infty}$ = Class $C^{\infty}$*) A property of a real-valued function (or map between Euclidean spaces): all partial derivatives of all orders exist and are continuous.

- $f$ is $C^{\infty}$ on an open set $U \subseteq \mathbb{R}^{n}$ .

where

- $C^{k}$ means continuous derivatives up to order $k$; $C^{\infty}$ is the intersection of all $C^{k}$.
- “smooth” and “infinitely differentiable” are used interchangeably.

</i>

<i>

**definition [d]** (*Smooth = Infinitely Differentiable = $C^{\infty}$*) In differential geometry, a structure whose defining maps have $C^{\infty}$ coordinate expressions:

- (Manifold) transition maps $\varphi_{\beta} \circ \varphi_{\alpha}^{-1}$ are $C^{\infty}$ .
- (Map $f: M \rightarrow N$) $\psi \circ f \circ \varphi^{-1}$ is $C^{\infty}$ in every pair of charts .
- (Tensor field) component functions in every chart are $C^{\infty}$ functions of the coordinates .

where

- $\varphi, \psi$ are local charts on $M$ and $N$.
- smoothness is chart-independent once the atlas is smooth.

</i>
