# Smooth Assignment

<i>

**definition [d]** (*Smooth Assignment = Smooth Section*) A mapping that attaches a type-$(r,s)$ tensor to each point so the assignment is smooth: for $U \subset M$,

- $T : U \rightarrow T^{r}_{\ s}(M)$ ,
- $T(P) \equiv T_{P} \in T^{r}_{\ s,P}(M)$ ,

with component functions of class $C^{\infty}$ in every chart.

where

- $M$ is a smooth manifold.
- $U$ is a subset of $M$.
- $T$ is the assignment or tensor field.
- $P$ is a point of $M$.
- $T^{r}_{\ s}(M)$ is the bundle of type-$(r,s)$ tensors on $M$.
- $T^{r}_{\ s,P}(M)$ is the space of type-$(r,s)$ tensors at $P$.
- $r$ is the contravariant degree.
- $s$ is the covariant degree.
- $C^{\infty}$ means infinitely differentiable.

Note:

- this is the definition of a smooth tensor field of type $(r,s)$.

</i>

<i>

**definition [d]** (*Smooth Assignment = Cross Section = Section*) A differentiable map $s: M \rightarrow E$ into a vector or tensor bundle that lands in the fiber over each point:

- $\pi \circ s = \mathrm{id}_{M}$ ,

so $s(p) \in E_{p} = \pi^{-1}(p)$ for every $p \in M$.

where

- $M$ is the base manifold.
- $E$ is the total space of the bundle.
- $\pi: E \rightarrow M$ is the bundle projection.
- $s$ is the section.
- $\mathrm{id}_{M}$ is the identity map on $M$.
- $E_{p} = \pi^{-1}(p)$ is the fiber over $p$.
- $p$ is a point of $M$.

Note:

- a smooth section of $T^{r}_{\ s}(M)$ is precisely a smooth type-$(r,s)$ tensor field.

</i>
