# Indefinite Integral

<i>

**definition [d]** (*Indefinite Integral = Antiderivative = Primitive*) The most general antiderivative of $f$, written

- $\displaystyle \int f(x)\, dx = F(x) + C$ ,

where $F'(x) = f(x)$ and $C$ is an arbitrary constant of integration.

where

- $f$ is the integrand.
- $F$ is any antiderivative of $f$.
- $C$ is the constant of integration.
- the indefinite integral denotes a family of functions, not a single number.

</i>
